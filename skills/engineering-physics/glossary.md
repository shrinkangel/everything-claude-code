# Glossary — Engineering Physics

Alphabetical index of significant terms across all 26 chapters. Format: **Term -- definition (Ch N)**.

---

## A

- **Absorption Coefficient (α)** -- ratio of sound energy absorbed by a surface to total incident energy; 0 ≤ α ≤ 1. (Ch 10)
- **Acceptance Angle (2θₐ)** -- the cone of incident angles that propagate by total internal reflection in an optical fiber. (Ch 22)
- **Acceptor Atom / Acceptor Level (Eₐ)** -- trivalent (group III) impurity that accepts an electron from the valence band, leaving a hole. (Ch 26)
- **Allotropy** -- multiple crystal forms of a single element (e.g., carbon: diamond, graphite, graphene, fullerene). (Ch 18)
- **Ampère's Circuital Law** -- line integral of B around a closed loop equals μ₀ times enclosed current; differential form ∇×B = μ₀J. (Ch 5)
- **Anomalous Dispersion** -- n decreases with increasing ω in the neighborhood of ω₀; outside this region n increases (normal dispersion). (Ch 21)
- **Antisite Defect** -- in an ordered compound, atom A sits on a B site. (Ch 16)
- **Anechoic (Dead) Room** -- every surface has α = 1; perfect absorber. (Ch 10)
- **Atomic Packing Factor (APF)** -- ratio of volume of atoms in a unit cell to volume of cell; SC 0.52, BCC 0.68, FCC = HCP 0.74. (Ch 15)
- **Attenuation Coefficient (γ)** -- exponential decay constant of intensity; γ = 2/δ for conductor (δ = skin depth). (Ch 21)

## B

- **Band Gap (E_g)** -- forbidden energy range between valence band top E_V and conduction band bottom E_C. (Ch 25, Ch 26)
- **Bardeen–Cooper–Schrieffer (BCS) Theory** -- phonon-mediated attractive interaction binds two electrons into a Cooper pair; explains T_C, isotope effect, energy gap. (Ch 20)
- **Beer's Law (Linear Absorption)** -- I(x) = I₀ e^(−μx); mass absorption μ/ρ = kλ³Z³ between absorption edges. (Ch 17)
- **Birefringence (Double Refraction)** -- anisotropic crystal splits incident ray into O-ray (spherical wavefront) and E-ray (ellipsoidal wavefront). (Ch 4)
- **Blackbody** -- idealized body absorbing all incident radiation; a small hole in a hollow cavity is a practical realization. (Ch 7)
- **Bloch Function u_k(x)** -- periodic part of the wavefunction; u_k(x+a) = u_k(x). (Ch 25)
- **Bloch's Theorem** -- eigenfunctions of a periodic potential are ψ_k(x) = u_k(x) e^(ikx). (Ch 25)
- **Bloch Wall (Domain Boundary)** -- boundary ~10⁻² μm thick between regions of aligned spin in a ferromagnet. (Ch 19)
- **Bohr Magneton (μ_B)** -- μ_B = eħ/(2m) = 9.27×10⁻²⁴ A·m²; fundamental unit of atomic magnetic moment. (Ch 19)
- **Bohr Model (Hydrogen)** -- L = nħ quantizes orbital angular momentum; produces full hydrogen spectrum. (Ch 7)
- **Boundary Conditions (EM)** -- B₁ₙ = B₂ₙ, E₁ₜ = E₂ₜ, H₁ₜ = H₂ₜ; D₁ₙ − D₂ₙ = σ_f. (Ch 6)
- **Bragg's Law** -- 2d sin θ = nλ; constraint λ < 2d for diffraction. (Ch 15, Ch 17)
- **Brillouin Function** -- quantum analog of Langevin function; B_J(a) with discrete m_J. (Ch 19)
- **Brillouin-Zone Boundary** -- k = ±nπ/a, where E(k) is discontinuous. (Ch 25)
- **Burgers Vector (b)** -- closure-failure vector of a Burgers circuit around a dislocation; defines edge (b ⊥ t̂) vs screw (b ∥ t̂). (Ch 16)

## C

- **Capacitance-Equivalent in Mechanical System** -- spring constant k in mechanical ODE maps to inverse capacitance 1/C. (Ch 1)
- **Cavitation** -- formation and implosion of vapor bubbles in a liquid under acoustic rarefaction; transient regime >10 W/cm² drives sonochemistry. (Ch 11)
- **Characteristic X-ray Spectrum** -- sharp lines from inner-shell ionization followed by outer-electron transitions (K_α, K_β). (Ch 17)
- **Clausius–Mossotti Relation** -- (εᵣ−1)/(εᵣ+2) = Nα/(3ε₀); bridges atomic polarizability and macroscopic permittivity. (Ch 23)
- **Coherence (Temporal/Spatial)** -- stable phase relation over time/spatial extent; precondition for interference. (Ch 1, Ch 2, Ch 14)
- **Coherence Length** -- maximum path difference over which a source still produces stable interference fringes. (Ch 14)
- **Colour Centre** -- defect producing optical absorption bands in transparent crystals. (Ch 16)
- **Complex Refractive Index n_c = n − ib** -- real n governs phase velocity, imaginary b governs attenuation. (Ch 21)
- **Compton Wavelength (λ_C)** -- λ_C = h/(m₀c) = 0.02426 Å for electrons; max Compton shift is 2λ_C. (Ch 7)
- **Continuous (Bremsstrahlung) Spectrum** -- broad X-ray band from multi-step electron deceleration; λ_min = hc/(qV). (Ch 17)
- **Cooper Pair** -- two electrons bound by virtual phonon exchange; charge carrier of the superconducting state. (Ch 20)
- **Couplant** -- liquid (glycerine, oil) between ultrasonic probe and part; required because ultrasonic energy reflects at solid/air interface. (Ch 12)
- **Critical Excitation Voltage (V_K)** -- minimum tube voltage to knock out a K-shell electron. (Ch 17)
- **Critical Magnetic Field (B_C)** -- applied field above which superconductivity is destroyed; parabolic law B_C(T) = B_C0(1 − T²/T_C²). (Ch 20)
- **Critical Temperature (T_C)** -- temperature below which a material becomes superconducting (Ch 20) or ferroelectric polarization vanishes (Ch 23).
- **Crystal System** -- 7 categories of 3D lattices: cubic, tetragonal, orthorhombic, rhombohedral, hexagonal, monoclinic, triclinic. (Ch 15)
- **Curie's Law** -- χ_p = C/T for paramagnets; emerges from Langevin/Brillouin L(a) ≈ a/3 at high T. (Ch 19)
- **Curie–Weiss Law** -- χ_m = C/(T − T_C); applies above T_C for ferromagnets (offset T_C) or T_N for antiferromagnets. (Ch 19, Ch 23)
- **Cut-off Frequency (ν_c)** -- frequency above which a wave propagates in a plasma; equals ω_p/(2π). (Ch 6)

## D

- **Dee** -- hollow D-shaped half-cylinder in a cyclotron; alternately biased to give a kick in the gap. (Ch 13)
- **de Broglie Hypothesis** -- λ = h/p = h/(mv) for non-relativistic matter waves. (Ch 7)
- **Debye (Sub-force)** -- permanent dipole ↔ induced dipole; W ∝ −(μ²α)/r⁶, temperature-independent. (Ch 18)
- **Debye Relaxation** -- orientation polarization α₀(ω) = α₀(0)/(1 + iωτ); peak ε″ at ωτ = 1. (Ch 23)
- **Density of Energy States g(ε)** -- number of available quantum states per unit volume per unit energy; g(ε) = Cε^(1/2) for free electrons. (Ch 24)
- **Diamagnetism** -- universal, temperature-independent opposing moment; χ_dia = −μ₀NZe²⟨r²⟩/(6m). (Ch 19)
- **Dielectric Constant (εᵣ)** -- ratio ε/ε₀; capacitance multiplier when air is replaced by material. (Ch 23)
- **Dielectric Strength** -- minimum electric field at which breakdown occurs. (Ch 23)
- **Diffraction Grating Master Equation** -- (a+b) sin θ = nλ; resolving power R = nN. (Ch 3)
- **Displacement Current** -- I_d = ε₀ dΦ_E/dt; Maxwell's correction to Ampère's law, essential for EM waves. (Ch 5)
- **Donor Atom / Donor Level (E_d)** -- pentavalent (group V) impurity donating a loosely bound electron to the conduction band. (Ch 26)
- **Drift Tube** -- cylindrical electrode in a linac with length chosen so ion takes T/2 to cross. (Ch 13)
- **Drude–Lorentz Free Electron Model** -- electrons as classical particles colliding with lattice; σ = Ne²τ/m. (Ch 24)
- **Duane–Hunt Limit** -- λ_min = hc/(qV) = 12400 Å·V/V; highest-frequency cutoff of X-ray spectrum. (Ch 17)

## E

- **Echelon Effect** -- regular step-like surfaces produce periodic echoes that fuse into a musical tone. (Ch 10)
- **Eddy Current** -- AC-driven coil induces eddy currents in conductor; flaws register as impedance changes. (Ch 12)
- **Effective Bohr Magneton Number** -- p = g√(J(J+1)); experimentally determined from χ. (Ch 19)
- **Effective Mass (m\*)** -- band electron response to force; replaces bare m in semiconductor formulas. (Ch 26)
- **Eigenvalue Equation** -- Âψ = aψ where a is the allowed measured value of observable A. (Ch 7)
- **Einstein's Postulates of Special Relativity** -- (1) laws of physics same in all inertial frames; (2) c is constant. (Ch 9)
- **Einstein A/B Coefficients** -- rate equations for stimulated absorption, spontaneous emission, stimulated emission; A₂₁/B₂₁ = 8πhν³/c³. (Ch 22)
- **Electric Displacement D = ε₀E + P** -- separates free charge from polarization charge. (Ch 23)
- **Electronegativity** -- tendency of an atom to attract electrons; high difference ⇒ ionic, similar ⇒ covalent. (Ch 18)
- **Energy Band** -- range of electron energies permitted inside a solid; arises from N overlapping atomic levels. (Ch 25)
- **Energy Gap (BCS)** -- minimum energy to break a Cooper pair; ~3.53 k_B T_C at T=0. (Ch 20)
- **Equipartition (Classical Failure)** -- Drude model fails specific heat because thermal energy affects only ~kT/ε_F of electrons. (Ch 24)
- **Evanescent Wave** -- decaying solution ψ ∝ e^(−k₁x) in classically forbidden region; carries no net flux. (Ch 8)
- **Ewald Sphere** -- geometric construct in reciprocal space showing which reciprocal-lattice points satisfy Bragg's condition. (Ch 17)
- **Expectation Value ⟨A⟩** -- weighted average ⟨A⟩ = ∫ψ* Âψ dV. (Ch 7)

## F

- **Faraday's Law of Induction** -- emf = −dΦ/dt; integral form ∮E·dℓ = −(d/dt)∫B·ds. (Ch 5)
- **Fermi–Dirac Distribution F(ε)** -- F(ε) = 1/(e^((ε−ε_F)/kT) + 1); the +1 from Pauli distinguishes it from Bose/MB. (Ch 24, Ch 26)
- **Fermi Energy (ε_F)** -- highest occupied level at 0 K; energy with 50% occupation at any T. (Ch 24)
- **Fermi Velocity (v_F)** -- speed of electrons near Fermi level; v_F² = 2ε_F0/m. (Ch 24)
- **Fermi's Golden Rule (Rate Equation)** -- Einstein A/B rates reproduce Planck only with stimulated emission. (Ch 22)
- **Ferroelectric Hysteresis** -- P(E) loop with P_sat, P_R, E_C; bistable polarization below T_C. (Ch 23)
- **Flux Pinning** -- trapping of magnetic flux at defects in type-II superconductor; enables levitation. (Ch 20)
- **Focussing (Acoustic)** -- cylindrical/spherical wall concavities concentrate reflections at a point. (Ch 10)
- **Forbidden Energy Band** -- range of energies where no electron state exists. (Ch 25)
- **Four-Level Laser** -- He-Ne, Nd:YAG; add a lower level E′ above ground so inversion N_E1 > N_E′ is easy. (Ch 22)
- **Fraunhofer Diffraction** -- source and screen at infinity; far-field pattern is Fourier transform of aperture. (Ch 3)
- **Free Electron Model** -- ion-core potential taken uniform; electrons free throughout solid. (Ch 24)
- **Frenkel Defect** -- vacancy + interstitial of the same ion (almost always the cation). (Ch 16)
- **Frequency (ν)** -- inverse of period; T = 1/ν. (Ch 1)
- **Fresnel Diffraction** -- source/screen at finite distance; intensity computed by half-period zones. (Ch 3)
- **Fresnel's Half-Period Zones** -- concentric annular zones with consecutive edges differing by λ/2; n-th zone amplitude R_n ∝ (1 + cos θ_n). (Ch 3)
- **Fresnel Reflectivity** -- R = ((n₂−n₁)/(n₂+n₁))² for dielectrics at normal incidence. (Ch 21)

## G

- **Gabor Zone Plate** -- interference pattern from reference and object waves; the in-line hologram. (Ch 14)
- **Gauss's Law of Electrostatics** -- ∮E·n̂ ds = q/ε₀; in dielectrics ∮D·ds = q'. (Ch 5)
- **Glan–Taylor / Nicol Prism (implied)** -- polarization by birefringence. (Ch 4)
- **Grain Boundary** -- interface between crystallites of differing orientation; width ~few atomic diameters. (Ch 16)
- **Grating Dispersion** -- dθ/dλ = n/[(a+b) cos θ]; angular spread per wavelength change. (Ch 3)
- **Group Velocity v_g = dω/dk** -- speed of the envelope/signal/energy. (Ch 1, Ch 7)

## H

- **Hall Coefficient R_H** -- R_H = 3π/(8Ne); negative for n-type, positive for p-type. (Ch 24)
- **Hall Effect** -- transverse E developed perpendicular to current and B; sign identifies carrier type. (Ch 24)
- **Hamaker Constant (A)** -- macroscopic effective VdW parameter; ~10⁻²⁰ to 10⁻¹⁹ J. (Ch 18)
- **Hamiltonian Ĥ** -- operator for total energy; Ĥ = −(ħ²/2m)∇² + V. (Ch 7)
- **Heisenberg Exchange Integral J_e** -- positive ⇒ ferromagnetic, negative ⇒ antiferromagnetic; the quantum origin of magnetism. (Ch 19)
- **Heisenberg Uncertainty Principle** -- Δx·Δp ≥ ħ/2; also ΔE·Δt ≥ ħ/2 and ΔJ·Δθ ≥ ħ/2. (Ch 7)
- **Hooke's Law F = −kx** -- linear restoring force; foundation of all Ch 1 dynamics. (Ch 1)
- **Huygens' Principle** -- every point on a wavefront is itself a source of secondary spherical wavelets. (Ch 2, Ch 3)
- **Hydrogen Bond Length (water)** -- ~197 pm; up to 4 H-bonds per molecule (2 lone pairs + 2 H). (Ch 18)

## I

- **Inertial Frame** -- Newton's first law holds; no experiment detects absolute velocity. (Ch 9)
- **Interplanar Spacing d_{hkl}** -- cubic: d = a/√(h²+k²+ℓ²). (Ch 15)
- **Interstitial** -- extra atom in non-lattice position; common impurities: C, N, H, O. (Ch 16)
- **Intrinsic Semiconductor** -- pure crystal where carriers come only from thermally broken bonds; n = p = n_i. (Ch 26)
- **Ionization Energy / Potential** -- energy to remove electron from gaseous atom. (Ch 18)

## J

- **Josephson Junction** -- two superconductors separated by ~10–20 Å insulator; Cooper pairs tunnel. (Ch 20)

## K

- **K-Absorption Edge (λ_K)** -- discontinuity in μ/ρ at K-ionization energy; used to filter K_β line. (Ch 17)
- **Keesom (Sub-force)** -- permanent dipole ↔ permanent dipole; W ∝ −μ₁²μ₂²/(k_B T · r⁶). (Ch 18)
- **Kronig–Penney Model** -- 1D square-well periodic potential; dispersion constraint (P sin k₀a)/(k₀a) + cos k₀a = cos ka. (Ch 25)

## L

- **Lamellar Field** -- ∇×F = 0; derivable from scalar potential. (Ch 5)
- **Langevin Function L(a)** -- classical paramagnetic theory; L(a) ≈ a/3 at small a. (Ch 19)
- **Larmor Frequency ω_L = eB/(2m)** -- precession frequency of orbital moment about applied field. (Ch 19)
- **Lattice + Basis Construction** -- primitive translation vectors + basis of atoms; defines crystal structure. (Ch 15)
- **Length Contraction** -- ℓ = ℓ₀/γ; only along direction of motion. (Ch 9)
- **Lenz's Law** -- induced emf opposes its cause (negative sign in Faraday). (Ch 5)
- **Light Vector (E)** -- electric vector of EM wave; dominates optical phenomena. (Ch 4, Ch 21)
- **Line Integral / Circulation** -- ∫F·dℓ; work done if F is force. (Ch 5)
- **London Equations** -- two-fluid model: dJ_s/dt = (N_s e²/m*)E and ∇×J_s = −(N_s e²/m*)B. (Ch 20)
- **London Penetration Depth (λ)** -- characteristic distance over which external B decays to 1/e inside a superconductor. (Ch 20)
- **Longitudinal Coherence Length** -- path-difference budget over which interference fringes remain stable. (Ch 14)
- **Lorentz Force / Transformation** -- x′ = γ(x − vt), t′ = γ(t − vx/c²), y′ = y, z′ = z. (Ch 9)
- **Lorentz Number (L)** -- L = π²k²/(3e²) ≈ 2.45×10⁻⁸ WΩK⁻²; constant ratio K/(σT). (Ch 24)
- **Loss Tangent tan δ = ε″/ε′** -- ratio of imaginary to real dielectric response. (Ch 23)

## M

- **Magnetic Susceptibility χ_m = M/B** -- sign is the first classifier of magnetic material. (Ch 19)
- **Magnetostriction** -- ferromagnetic materials change length in applied field; drives at 2× the AC frequency. (Ch 11)
- **Malus's Law** -- I = I₀ cos²θ for polarizer-analyzer pair. (Ch 4)
- **Mass–Energy Equivalence** -- E = γm₀c² = K + m₀c²; m₀c² is rest energy. (Ch 9)
- **Maxwell's Equations (Differential)** -- ∇·D = ρ, ∇·B = 0, ∇×E = −∂B/∂t, ∇×H = J + ∂D/∂t. (Ch 5)
- **Meissner Effect** -- expulsion of magnetic flux from superconductor interior; χ = −1, defining property of superconducting state. (Ch 20)
- **Metastable State** -- long-lived excited level (τ ~ 10⁻³ s) that acts as upper laser level. (Ch 22)
- **Mie / Tyndall Scattering** -- particle size ≳ λ; explains white clouds and fog. (Ch 21)
- **Miller Indices (hkl)** -- reciprocals of fractional intercepts on crystallographic axes. (Ch 15)
- **Mobility μ = v/E** -- drift speed per unit applied field. (Ch 24, Ch 26)
- **Modal Dispersion** -- pulse spreading because different modes take different optical paths. (Ch 22)
- **Modal Dispersion Mitigation (Graded Index)** -- parabolic n(r) so peripheral rays travel faster. (Ch 22)
- **Modulus (Young's Y, Shear G, etc.)** -- material constants setting ultrasonic velocities in solids. (Ch 12)
- **Mössbauer / Moseley's Law** -- √ν ∝ (Z − σ); identifies element from K_α line. (Ch 17)

## N

- **Néel Temperature (T_N)** -- transition temperature for antiferromagnets; susceptibility peaks at T_N. (Ch 19)
- **Numerical Aperture (NA)** -- NA = √(n₁² − n₂²) = n₁√(2Δ); quantifies fiber light-gathering. (Ch 22)

## O

- **Obliquity Factor (1 + cos θ)/2** -- directional weighting of secondary wavelets. (Ch 3)
- **Operator Â** -- linear mapping satisfying Â(a₁ψ₁ + a₂ψ₂) = a₁Âψ₁ + a₂Âψ₂; momentum = −iħ ∂/∂x. (Ch 7)
- **Optical Activity** -- ability of certain substances to rotate the plane of polarization. (Ch 4)
- **Optical Path** -- μ × geometric path; refractive index adjusts for phase counting. (Ch 2)
- **Optical Resonator** -- two parallel mirrors, mλ/2 = L, one fully reflecting, one partially transmitting. (Ch 22)
- **Ostwald's Step Rule** -- least stable polymorph crystallizes first, converts to more stable over time. (Ch 18)

## P

- **Packing Factor** -- see Atomic Packing Factor. (Ch 15)
- **Phase Velocity v_p = ω/k** -- speed of a single sinusoid's crest. (Ch 1, Ch 7)
- **Phonon** -- quantized lattice vibration; created/absorbed in indirect transitions to conserve momentum. (Ch 20, Ch 21)
- **Photon-Energy–Wavelength Conversion** -- λ(Å) = 12400/E(eV). (Ch 22)
- **Piezoelectric Effect** -- mechanical strain on non-centrosymmetric crystal produces charge; converse: voltage → strain. (Ch 11)
- **Plane of Polarization / Vibration** -- contains B and k (polarization) or E and k (vibration); perpendicular planes. (Ch 4)
- **Plasma Frequency ω_p** -- ω_p = √(Ne²/(ε₀m)); high-pass filter for wave propagation. (Ch 6)
- **Pockels / Kerr Effect (implied)** -- electro-optic index change in non-centrosymmetric crystals. (Ch 4)
- **Polaron** -- charge carrier + its self-induced lattice polarization; effective mass > free electron. (Ch 16)
- **Polarizability α = μₑ/E_L** -- atomic-scale property; α = αₑ + αᵢ + α₀ (electronic + ionic + orientation). (Ch 23)
- **Polarization (Dielectric) P = Nμₑ** -- dipole moments per unit volume; equals induced surface charge density. (Ch 23)
- **Polymorphism** -- multiple crystal forms of a compound (e.g., HgI₂ orthorhombic/trigonal). (Ch 18)
- **Population Inversion** -- N₂ > N₁; prerequisite for lasing. (Ch 22)
- **Poynting Vector P = E×H** -- instantaneous EM power density (W/m²). (Ch 6)
- **Primary Bonds** -- ionic, covalent, metallic; involve changes in electron ownership. (Ch 18)
- **Probability Density |ψ|²** -- probability of finding particle in dV is |ψ|² dV. (Ch 7)
- **Pulse–Echo Round Trip** -- ℓ = (1/2) v Δt for one-way distance. (Ch 11, Ch 12)

## Q

- **Quality Factor Q = ω₁/(2γ)** -- 2π × (energy stored / energy lost per cycle). (Ch 1)
- **Quantum Efficiency** -- ratio of emitted photons to electrons crossing junction in an LED. (Ch 22)

## R

- **Rayleigh Criterion** -- two point sources resolvable when principal maxima are separated by first zero of each other's pattern. (Ch 3)
- **Rayleigh Scattering** -- I_scat ∝ 1/λ⁴ when particle size ≪ λ; explains blue sky, red sunsets. (Ch 21)
- **Reactive Muffler** -- reflects via impedance mismatch; good at low f, narrow-band. (Ch 10)
- **Reconstruction (Holography)** -- re-illuminating developed plate with reference wave alone. (Ch 14)
- **Reciprocal Lattice** -- vector space where G_{hkl} encodes both orientation and spacing of planes; |G| = 2π/d_{hkl}. (Ch 15)
- **Rectilinear Propagation** -- limit of diffraction when apertures >> λ. (Ch 3)
- **Reflection Coefficient R = (k − k₀)²/(k + k₀)²** -- quantum potential step with E > V₀. (Ch 8)
- **Reflective Hologram** -- reference/object waves enter opposite sides; viewable in white light. (Ch 14)
- **Refractive Index n = c/v** -- = √(μᵣεᵣ) at same frequency. (Ch 6)
- **Relativistic Velocity Addition** -- u_x = (u_x′ + v)/(1 + u_x′v/c²); enforces c as hard cap. (Ch 9)
- **Resonance** -- driving frequency matches natural frequency; amplitude peaks. (Ch 1)
- **Reverberation Time T (Sabine)** -- T = 0.165V/∑α_i s_i; time for 60 dB drop. (Ch 10)
- **Rotating-Wave / Brillouin Limit** -- B_J(a) → L(a) as J → ∞. (Ch 19)

## S

- **Sabin (O.W.U.)** -- 1 Sabin = absorption of 1 m² of open window. (Ch 10)
- **Sabine's Reverberation Formula** -- T = 0.165V/∑α_i s_i. (Ch 10)
- **Scalar Field** -- assigns scalar to every point; vector field assigns vector. (Ch 5)
- **Scattering (Rayleigh, Mie, Compton)** -- wavelength-dependent interactions from sub-λ, supra-λ, and X-ray regimes. (Ch 7, Ch 21)
- **Schottky Defect** -- paired cation + anion vacancies preserving charge neutrality. (Ch 16)
- **Schrödinger Equation (Time-Independent)** -- d²ψ/dx² + (2m/ħ²)(E − V)ψ = 0. (Ch 7, Ch 8)
- **Secondary Bonds** -- hydrogen, van der Waals; weaker inter-molecular. (Ch 18)
- **Skin Depth (δ)** -- δ = √(2/(μσω)); penetration depth of EM wave in conductor. (Ch 21)
- **Solenoidal Field** -- ∇·F = 0; derivable from vector potential. (Ch 5)
- **Sommerfeld Quantum Free Electron Model** -- electrons as 3D infinite potential box; fill per Pauli. (Ch 24)
- **Sonar** -- Sound Navigation And Ranging; passive (listen) vs active (ping + echo). (Ch 11)
- **Specific Rotation ω** -- rotation per 10 cm of solution with 1 g/cm³ active substance; units deg·cm²/g. (Ch 4)
- **Stationary Wave** -- counter-propagating identical waves; fixed nodes/antinodes, no net energy flux. (Ch 1)
- **Step-Index Fiber NA** -- NA = n₁sin θₐ = √(n₁² − n₂²) = n₁√(2Δ). (Ch 22)
- **Stokes' Relation** -- reflection at denser medium adds π phase shift; rarer medium adds nothing. (Ch 2)
- **Stopping Potential V₀** -- retarding potential that just stops most energetic photoelectrons. (Ch 7)
- **Superconducting Quantum Interference Device (SQUID)** -- two Josephson junctions in parallel; most sensitive magnetic flux sensor. (Ch 20)
- **Surface Integral / Flux** -- ∫F·n̂ ds; number of field lines crossing S. (Ch 5)

## T

- **Three-Level Laser** -- ruby; pump E₀ → E₂, accumulate in metastable E₁, transition E₁ → E₀. (Ch 22)
- **Threshold Frequency ν₀** -- minimum frequency below which no photoelectric emission occurs. (Ch 7)
- **Time Dilation** -- Δt = γΔt₀; proper time Δt₀ is measured in clock's rest frame. (Ch 9)
- **Time-Independent Schrödinger Equation** -- the workhorse; apply four boundary conditions. (Ch 8)
- **Total Internal Reflection (Fiber)** -- only rays with θ < θₐ propagate; acceptance angle = 2θₐ. (Ch 22)
- **Transition Types** -- direct inter-band, indirect inter-band (phonon required), intra-band (metals, IR). (Ch 21)
- **Transmission Coefficient T = j_T/j_I** -- flux ratio; R + T = 1 always. (Ch 8)
- **Transmission Hologram** -- reference/object waves enter same side at small angle; requires laser to view. (Ch 14)
- **Transverse Electromagnetic (TEM) Wave** -- E·k = 0, H·k = 0, k×E = μωH. (Ch 6)
- **Tunneling** -- non-zero probability to traverse classically forbidden barrier of finite width. (Ch 8)
- **Two Coherent-Source Classes** -- division of wavefront vs division of amplitude. (Ch 2)
- **Type-I Superconductor** -- complete flux expulsion until abrupt collapse at single B_C; low B_C ≤ 0.1 T. (Ch 20)
- **Type-II Superconductor** -- flux penetrates as vortices between B_C1 and B_C2; only type for high-field magnets. (Ch 20)

## U

- **Ultrasound** -- ν > 20 kHz; NDT band typically 0.5–25 MHz. (Ch 11, Ch 12)
- **Unit Cell** -- smallest cell whose translation generates entire lattice; 6 lattice parameters. (Ch 15)

## V

- **Vacancy** -- missing atom at a regular lattice site; equilibrium concentration N_v = N exp(−Q_v/kT). (Ch 16)
- **Van der Waals Forces** -- Keesom + Debye + London; all decay as 1/r⁶. (Ch 18)
- **Vortex State** -- mixed state of type-II between B_C1 and B_C2; magnetic flux tubes threading normal-conducting cylinders. (Ch 20)

## W

- **Wave Packet** -- spatially localized envelope built from a band of k's; carries information. (Ch 1)
- **Wavefront** -- surface of constant phase; spherical near point sources, plane far away. (Ch 2)
- **Weiss Molecular-Field Theory** -- B_T = B_e + λM; gives χ_m = C/(T − T_C). (Ch 19)
- **Wiedemann–Franz Law** -- K/(σT) = L universal because heat and charge carried by same particles. (Ch 24)
- **Work Function (w₀)** -- minimum energy to liberate electron from metal at 0 K. (Ch 7)

## X

- **X-Ray Spectrum** -- continuous hill (Bremsstrahlung) + characteristic spikes (K_α, K_β lines). (Ch 17)

## Y

- **Young's Modulus / Slits** -- see Hooke's Law analog; slit spacing β = λD/2d. (Ch 2)

## Z

- **Zero-Point Energy** -- minimum non-zero energy E₁ ≠ 0 for a confined quantum particle. (Ch 8)
- **Zone Plate** -- alternate Fresnel zones blocked; acts as a chromatic lens. (Ch 3)
